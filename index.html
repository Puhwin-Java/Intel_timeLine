Intel is founded, later becoming a leader in technology innovation that enables more energy-efficient computing worldwide.
1968

Intel Founded

Intel releases the first commercial microprocessor (Intel 4004). This marks the start of major computing growth, which later increases Intel’s responsibility to manage environmental impacts from scaling production.
1971

Intel 4004

Intel continues expanding manufacturing operations. During this period, Intel begins strengthening safety and environmental controls as fabs grow and chemical handling becomes a bigger issue.
1978

8086 Released

Intel grows as a major chip producer. Environmental and worker-safety practices become more important, with more structured handling of hazardous materials and factory waste.
1985

386 Microprocessor

Intel increases focus on formal environmental management as regulations and public concern rise. This helps set the stage for later sustainability reporting and long-term goals.
1993

Pentium Era

Intel begins major momentum in renewable energy use and sustainability reporting. This period marks Intel shifting from “compliance” to more active climate and sustainability leadership.
2006

Intel Core

Intel is recognized as a major corporate sustainability leader. It makes strong progress in renewable electricity, water recycling/restoration, and waste reduction programs across operations.
2015

2015 Milestone

Intel reinforces modern sustainability goals: stronger commitments to net-zero emissions, net positive water, and expanding sustainability requirements across the supply chain.
2022

2022 Milestone

About

Since its founding in 1968, Intel has grown from managing basic environmental and safety concerns to becoming a global sustainability leader. Over the decades, the company has focused on energy efficiency, renewable energy, water conservation and restoration, waste reduction, and reducing emissions. Today, Intel pursues ambitious goals like net-zero carbon emissions, net positive water use, and sustainable supply chain practices.
